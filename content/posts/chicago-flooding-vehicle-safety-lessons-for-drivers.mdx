Recent severe storms have wreaked havoc across the Chicago metropolitan area, transforming familiar roads into treacherous waterways and leaving a trail of stranded vehicles and closed major arteries. For car enthusiasts, tuners, and daily drivers alike, these events serve as a stark reminder of the immense power of nature and the critical importance of understanding how floodwaters can compromise both vehicle integrity and personal safety. From the Bishop Ford Freeway to suburban streets, the impact was widespread, underscoring the need for vigilance and preparedness when facing extreme weather conditions.

<Callout type="takeaway">
  Never attempt to drive through floodwaters; the risks to your vehicle and personal safety are far too great.
</Callout>

## What happened
Overnight storms brought significant rainfall to the Chicago area, leading to widespread flooding that severely impacted infrastructure and daily life. The Bishop Ford Freeway, a major thoroughfare, was closed in both directions for much of Saturday due to high water. Numerous suburbs, including Naperville, Elgin, and Bartlett, reported streets submerged, resulting in multiple vehicles becoming stuck after drivers attempted to navigate through the floodwaters. One Naperville motorist recounted how quickly the water rose, catching him off guard despite initial warnings.

Beyond vehicular issues, the storms caused broader disruptions, including power outages for thousands and the postponement of some Fourth of July events, such as a Cubs-Cardinals game. Tragically, a 47-year-old woman and her two dogs died in Kendall County when a tree fell on their camper trailer. These incidents collectively painted a grim picture of the storm's intensity and its far-reaching consequences across the region.

## Why it matters
For anyone passionate about their vehicle, the sight of cars stranded in deep water is a nightmare. Floodwaters can cause catastrophic damage to a car's engine, electrical system, and interior, often leading to a total loss. Beyond the financial implications, the personal danger of becoming trapped in a rapidly rising flood is immense. These events highlight how quickly seemingly navigable roads can become deadly traps, especially for those who underestimate the depth or force of moving water. It's a critical lesson that even the most robust build or daily driver is vulnerable to the elements, emphasizing that no car is truly flood-proof.

## How to think about it
When faced with flood warnings or visible standing water, the safest and most responsible approach is always to turn around. Even a few inches of water can stall a vehicle, and deeper water can sweep it away. Before heading out during or after heavy rainfall, check local weather advisories and road conditions. Plan alternate routes that avoid known flood-prone areas. If you encounter a flooded road unexpectedly, do not attempt to drive through it. Remember that the depth of water can be deceptive, and hidden hazards like potholes or debris can cause further damage or loss of control. Prioritize your safety and the longevity of your vehicle by exercising extreme caution.

<Callout type="warning">
  Never underestimate the power of floodwaters. Six inches of water can reach the bottom of most passenger cars, causing loss of control or stalling. Twelve inches can float many vehicles, and two feet of rushing water can carry away most cars, including SUVs and pickups. "Turn around, don't drown." is not just a slogan—it's a life-saving directive.
</Callout>
