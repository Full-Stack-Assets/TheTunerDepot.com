The John Deere Classic delivered its usual blend of drama and unexpected turns, with two-time champion Jordan Spieth battling fiercely to make the cut on the number after a shaky start to his second round. While Spieth ultimately secured his weekend play with a clutch back-nine performance, rising star Jackson Koivun, making his professional debut, unfortunately fell short. This year's tournament at TPC Deere Run also saw significant course changes, particularly to the par-4 fourth hole, which added an extra layer of challenge for the field and contributed to a higher cutline compared to previous years.

## What happened
Jordan Spieth, a two-time winner at the John Deere Classic, found himself in a precarious position during the second round. After a pulled iron shot on the par-5 10th led to a bogey, he needed a strong finish. Spieth managed to card three crucial back-nine birdies, ultimately making the cut at 3-under par, exactly on the number. His recovery was a testament to his resilience, even as he admitted to struggling with his swing earlier in the day.

Conversely, Jackson Koivun, the former top-ranked amateur making his professional debut, couldn't replicate the form that saw him finish T11 at the same event a year prior. Despite improving his score by three shots with a 1-under 70 on Friday, it wasn't enough, and he missed the cut. Other notable players who missed the cut included defending champion Brian Campbell and past John Deere winners Dylan Fritelli and Michael Kim.

Adding to the challenge, TPC Deere Run's par-4 fourth hole underwent a significant redesign, lengthening it by 50 yards and replacing a prominent tree with strategic bunkering. This change dramatically increased the hole's difficulty, playing to an average of 4.199 through two rounds, making it the second most challenging hole on the course. This altered dynamic likely contributed to the cutline being higher than the record-low 5-under par seen in the previous two years.

## Why it matters
Spieth's narrow escape underscores the competitive intensity of the PGA TOUR, demonstrating that even top-tier players can face immense pressure and must fight for every shot. His ability to recover from a difficult position provides a valuable lesson in mental fortitude for aspiring golfers. For Koivun, missing the cut in his professional debut is a stark reminder of the transition from amateur to professional play, where consistency and mental toughness are paramount. It sets the stage for his continued development and pursuit of his first professional made cut at future events.

The course changes at TPC Deere Run also hold significant implications. By making a key hole more challenging, the tournament organizers have altered strategic play, forcing golfers to make tougher decisions and potentially rewarding more precise ball-stristrikers. This evolution of the course ensures the John Deere Classic remains a demanding test, influencing player strategies and potentially impacting scores across the field. It highlights how subtle changes to a course can dramatically shift the dynamics of a tournament.
