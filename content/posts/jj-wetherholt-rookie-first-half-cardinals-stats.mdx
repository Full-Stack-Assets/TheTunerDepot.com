His calm demeanor and consistent impact on the field have made him a standout for the Cardinals, even in hostile environments like Wrigley Field. This deep dive into his key statistics reveals just how valuable he's been across all facets of the game, solidifying his place as one of MLB's most promising young talents and a crucial asset for St. Louis.

## What happened
Through 83 games, JJ Wetherholt has accumulated an impressive 3.8 fWAR, ranking him sixth best in Major League Baseball and third in the National League. This comprehensive metric, which measures a player's total value across offense, defense, and baserunning compared to a replacement-level player, underscores his significant and balanced contributions to the Cardinals' success.

Offensively, Wetherholt has posted a .269/.364/.416 slash line, complemented by 13 home runs, 36 RBIs, and 54 runs scored. His performance at the plate has been 20% above MLB average by wRC+, providing consistent production from the leadoff spot as a rookie. Defensively, he leads all defenders with 18 Outs Above Average (OAA) and ranks fifth in MLB with 14 Defensive Runs Saved (DRS), showcasing elite glove work at second base.

On the basepaths, Wetherholt has nine stolen bases, but his impact extends far beyond just swiping bags. He consistently creates runs by turning singles into doubles, doubles into triples, and taking extra bases when opportunities arise. This aggressive and intelligent baserunning has given him a +3 baserunning run value, tied for 12th among all MLB players, further highlighting his multifaceted value.
