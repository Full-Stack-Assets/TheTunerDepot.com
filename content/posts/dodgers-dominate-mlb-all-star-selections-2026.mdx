The Los Angeles Dodgers are making a significant statement in the 2026 MLB season, with four of their players selected as National League starters for the upcoming All-Star Game. This impressive showing, highlighted by fan-favorite Shohei Ohtani leading all players in votes, underscores the team's dominant performance and deep talent pool. With a total of five Dodgers heading to Philadelphia, their strong presence at the midsummer classic reflects not only individual excellence but also the collective strength of the back-to-back champions, setting a high bar for the rest of the league.

## What happened
Major League Baseball announced the rosters for the 2026 All-Star Game, revealing that the Los Angeles Dodgers will send four players to the NL's starting lineup. Shohei Ohtani, the leading vote-getter across all players, will start as the designated hitter. He is joined by teammates Freddie Freeman at first base, Max Muncy at third, and Andy Pages in the outfield. Additionally, right-hander Yoshinobu Yamamoto was selected as a reserve, bringing the Dodgers' total All-Stars to five, an MLB-best alongside the Atlanta Braves and Philadelphia Phillies.

The All-Star Game, scheduled for July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, will also feature a strong contingent from other top teams. The Braves and Phillies each matched the Dodgers with five All-Stars, while the Toronto Blue Jays, New York Yankees, and Tampa Bay Rays each boast four representatives in the American League. Notable AL starters include Ernie Clement, Junior Caminero, Bobby Witt Jr., and Yordan Alvarez, with Nick Kurtz stepping in for Vladimir Guerrero Jr. after a withdrawal. This year's rosters also highlight a star-studded rookie class, with players like Travis Bazzana and Parker Messick making the team.
