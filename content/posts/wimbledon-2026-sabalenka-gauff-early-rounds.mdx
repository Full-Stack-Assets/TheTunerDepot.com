## What happened
Aryna Sabalenka continued her impressive run at Wimbledon 2026, securing her third-round match's first set against Jelena Ostapenko with a single break of serve. Sabalenka's performance was marked by exceptional serving, landing 70% of her first serves, which proved difficult for Ostapenko to counter. Commentators noted Sabalenka's ability to recognize and exploit Ostapenko's tendency to cheat on her return position, consistently serving with pinpoint accuracy when it mattered most.

Meanwhile, Coco Gauff also claimed her first set against Claire Liu, but not without considerable effort. Gauff experienced a loose game early in the second set, giving up a 30-0 lead and facing break points. Liu displayed rapid footwork and teasing drop shots, pushing Gauff and forcing errors. It was highlighted on commentary that Gauff has been taken to three sets 20 times this season, suggesting a pattern of tough, drawn-out matches even when she ultimately prevails.

## Why it matters
Sabalenka's clinical performance against a powerful hitter like Ostapenko signals her strong contention for the title, demonstrating both physical prowess and tactical intelligence. Her tendency to go to three sets frequently could be a double-edged sword: it shows resilience but also suggests a potential vulnerability that could be exploited by top-tier opponents deeper into the tournament.
