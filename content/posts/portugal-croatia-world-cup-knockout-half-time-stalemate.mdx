## What happened
Portugal, who finished as runners-up in Group K, faced off against Croatia, second in Group L, in a crucial 2026 World Cup knockout match in Toronto. By halftime, the score remained 0-0, a testament to Croatia's defensive resolve against a Portuguese side that controlled the flow of the game.

Portugal exhibited significant attacking intent, maintaining a substantial possession advantage and completing a remarkable 313 passes compared to Croatia's 127. Despite this numerical superiority and creating several opportunities, including shots from Leao and near-misses from Ronaldo, Portugal's final touch proved blunt. Croatia, meanwhile, focused on packing their box and defending resolutely, with their squad appearing visibly tired but holding their ground against the relentless pressure.


## Why it matters
The outcome of this match carries immense weight, as the winner will advance to face Spain in Dallas on July 6, securing a coveted spot in the last 16. For Portugal, their inability to convert overwhelming possession into goals raises concerns about their clinical finishing in high-pressure situations. While their attacking movement and pressing were evident, a lack of decisive action could prove costly in a knockout tournament where every chance counts.

For Croatia, their defensive resilience is commendable, but the visible fatigue in their squad, including veteran Luka Modric being asked to conjure magic from deep, suggests a reliance on individual brilliance and a potentially unsustainable defensive strategy. If they are to progress, they will need to find a way to generate more meaningful attacks and alleviate the pressure on their backline, especially against future opponents who might be more adept at breaking down a packed defense.
